Undergraduate

In the autumn of 2016, I began my journalism studies at Xi'an International Studies University's School of Journalism and Communication.

Like many clueless high school graduates filling out college applications, I stumbled into media studies almost by accident - back then, I barely understood the field. Thankfully, instead of confining myself to dorm life, I joined the student union's publicity department. This decision unexpectedly connected me with lifelong friends while sharpening both technical and social skills through various projects. By my second year, I became the department director, eventually leading our team to win "Outstanding Department" honors.

Though I never developed a deep passion for journalism itself during these years, I grew to appreciate its nuances enough to dip my toes in the profession.

Graduation arrived in 2020 alongside nationwide pandemic lockdowns. Returning home, I entered local television through civil service exams. It was only when fully immersed in news reporting that the profession unveiled both its allure and challenges.
