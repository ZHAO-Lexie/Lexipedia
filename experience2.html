Hominid Upgrade Track

Working

In summer 2020, I became a journalist at Jinhua TV's Current Affairs Department. Initially shadowing senior reporters on investigative pieces – what I’d always considered "real journalism," though these were becoming both the most challenging and rarest stories to pursue in China’s media landscape. My mentor shared this conviction, though our department gradually phased out such reporting.

As experienced journalists transferred elsewhere, I pivoted to producing interview-based features, handling everything from planning to editing. Admittedly, this shift proved unexpectedly rewarding – fewer field assignments meant deeper dives into subjects through structured conversations.

Later, I joined our prime-time news broadcast team. Working on this flagship program demanded surgical precision, especially when covering government affairs. The high-stakes environment exposed me to bureaucratic complexities while connecting me with diverse officials and systems.

These three years transformed me: from handling entire production cycles (concept to distribution) independently, to evolving from an introverted rookie into someone actively seeking broader horizons. Journalism unexpectedly became my lens for understanding societal mechanisms – I craved witnessing more perspectives and exploring uncharted territories.

Yet witnessing traditional media’s decline became unavoidable. Our shrinking influence, innovation lag behind digital platforms, and barely sustainable revenue models ultimately prompted my decision to seek answers beyond this ecosystem.
